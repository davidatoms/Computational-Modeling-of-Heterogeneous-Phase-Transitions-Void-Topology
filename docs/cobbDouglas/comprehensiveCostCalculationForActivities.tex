\section{Introduction}
This analysis compares the total cost of producing 1,000 units with and without AI augmentation, accounting for direct costs, defect costs, and rework costs.

\section{Cost Calculations}
\subsection{Direct Costs}
\begin{align*}
\text{With AI:} & \quad \$8,000 (\text{Labor}) + \$1,000 (\text{AI}) = \boxed{\$9,000} \\
\text{Without AI:} & \quad \$10,000 (\text{Labor}) = \boxed{\$10,000}
\end{align*}

\subsection{Defect Costs}
\begin{align*}
\text{With AI:} & \quad (30 \times 10) + (40 \times 50) + (15 \times 200) = \boxed{\$5,300} \\
\text{Without AI:} & \quad (20 \times 10) + (30 \times 50) + (10 \times 200) = \boxed{\$3,700}
\end{align*}

\subsection{Rework Costs}
\begin{align*}
\text{With AI:} & \quad (50 \times 2 \times 25) + (50 \times 100) = \boxed{\$7,500} \\
\text{Without AI:} & \quad (17 \times 2 \times 25) + (17 \times 100) = \boxed{\$2,550}
\end{align*}

\subsection{Total Cost}
\begin{align*}
\text{With AI:} & \quad \$9,000 + \$5,300 + \$7,500 = \boxed{\$21,800} \\
\text{Without AI:} & \quad \$10,000 + \$3,700 + \$2,550 = \boxed{\$16,250} \\
\text{Net Difference:} & \quad \boxed{\$5,550} \text{ (AI is more expensive)}
\end{align*}

\section{Conclusion}
In this scenario, AI augmentation **increases total costs by \$5,550 per 1,000 units** due to higher defect and rework costs. Companies should carefully evaluate AI tools and workflows to ensure they deliver net benefits.
